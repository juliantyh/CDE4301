1. Introduction

This project is done in collaboration with DukeNUS and SGH to increase the adoption of endoscopic resection by Gastroenterologists for pre-cancerous gastric subepithelial lesions (Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors and Neuroendocrine Neoplasms) and early gastric cancer less than 3 cm, from 2% to 40% within 5 years, by achieving efficacy (R0 resection rates) and safety (complication rates) comparable to the gold standard of surgical resection and making the procedure easier, safer and faster. There are two main techniques for endoscopic submucosal resection. 1) Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD), the current standard-of-care resection technique where the endoscopist cuts into the mucosa, removes the lesion and closes the wound left behind 2) Endoscopic Full Thickness Resection (EFTR), using the OVESCO FTRD® System which is a 3-in-1 device that does a full-thickness or deep wall resection to grab hold of the lesion, resect it and close the wound left behind. Currently, In Singhealth, only SGH and CGH provide endoscopic resection procedures for sub-epithelial tumors. Additionally, only 2% of Gastroenterologists in the USA perform endoscopic resection of subepithelial lesions using Full Thickness Resection Device, the FTRD® System (XX, 2024). From our interviews at the SGH Ambulatory Endoscopy Centre (AEC), two endoscopists are confident to attempt the FTRD procedure with a high success rate. We ascertained that ESD and FTRD are complex and difficult procedure due to the following problems: 1) ESD is a time consuming and difficult procedure 2) During EFTR, it is difficult to determine if the lesion has been completely resected 3) During EFTR, it is difficult to ensure that the endoscopic clips close mucosal defects sufficiently to prevent blood leakage resulting in complications and need for hospitalization. Our device SafeCut™ makes the process of endoscopic resection of gastric subepithelial lesions and early gastric cancer just as safe and effective as surgical wedge resection, while being faster than the current standard-of-care endoscopic resection technique, Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD). It also improves on challenges faced by endoscopists when using the 3-in-1 FTRD® System to securely capture submucosal lesions and ensure complete resection, reducing the risk of perforation, while retaining the resection efficiency.

2. Problem Clarification

2.1. Stakeholder Analysis

From our interview with stakeholders at SGH, we have identified three main stakeholders: 1. Endoscopists want the resection of subepithelial lesions to safer and easier by ensuring that mucosal defects are closed, and the lesion is completely removed. 2. Patients find the bi-yearly screenings inconvenient and face stress and anxiety from living with tumours. They also want to remove them endoscopically in a single session. 3. Nurses take 1 year to master endoscopic procedure and >3 months to stabilize holding of the snare.

2.2. Target Audience

Primary Target Audience: Gastroenterologists who currently perform endoscopic resection of gastric subepithelial lesions and early gastric cancers. Secondary Target Audience: Physicians (other gastroenterologists, surgeons) who are trained in esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD) to diagnose gastric subepithelial lesions and early gastric cancers, but do not perform endoscopic resection.
